Bioinformatics is a combination of biology and computer science. Computer progarams help store, analyze, and present biological data. Proteins are a big part of biological processes. A single protein's actions can cause a cascade of other proteins' actions, which cause phenotypical results. Bioinformatics helps us to analyze that one starting protein by the adage, "structure confers reactivity." The 3D structure of a protein and its substrate are very important to what the protein does.
